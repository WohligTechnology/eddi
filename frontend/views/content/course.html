In what kinds of fields will you be active in the future…

Service Engineer

Car Dealership Mechanic

The work of a car dealership mechanic is automobile maintenance and repair at auto dealers, stable companies in various communities which make sales contracts with specific car manufacturers.

Heavy Machinery & Construction Equipment Mechanic

The work of this mechanic is the maintenance of heavy duty trucks, buses, and construction equipment such as cranes and forklifts. Unlike the case of car dealership mechanics, these mechanics’ customers are companies; so most of them have Saturdays and Sundays off.

Automotive Parts Dealer Mechanic

This type of mechanic specializes in giving cars custom dress-ups tailored for various customers, changing tires and oil, and installing car navigation systems and audio equipment as well as car inspection and vehicle maintenance checkups at dealers of automotive parts and auto accessories.

Motorcycle Dealer Mechanic

A motorcycle dealer mechanic specializes in motorcycle maintenance and repair at motorcycle dealers to perform motorcycle maintenance checkups and inspection, and install motorbike parts in response to various customer requests.

Crew at Customer Service Desk,
Plant Manager, and Salesperson

Big step-up in your career by giving full play to your abilities

As you gain experience as a mechanic at auto dealers, you can be promoted to a staff member dedicated to customer service and even to a plant manager if your skills and hard work are recognized. And you may be assigned to sales if your sales technique is appreciated. Most of auto dealers employ university graduates as salespersons; however, technical school graduates employed as mechanics can also aim to become salespersons. A staff member with great abilities may be promoted even to a store manager.
